December 19, 2023

CPSB Meeting

 

Members presentSteve Umling, Jasmine Green, Dr. Terence Vinson, Don Little,  Dr. Bonita Douzart, Mary Trammel, Darrin Dixon, Christine Tharpe, Barry Rachal, Katie McLain, Jessica Yeates, and Dottie Bell. Also present were Dr. T. Lamar Goree, Secretary, and Mr. Reggie Abrams, Board Counsel.

 

President Dr. Terence Vinson called the December 19, 2023, meeting to order, and Board Member Dottie Bell gave the Invocation and led the Pledge of Allegiance.

 

CONSIDERATION OF THE MINUTES OF THE NOVEMBER 7, 2023, NOVEMBER 20, 2023, AND NOVEMBER 28, 2023, CPSB MEETINGS. Mr. Dixon moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to approve the minutes of the November 7, 2023, November 20, 2023, and November 28, 2023, CPSB meetings as submitted in the electronic mailout.  Vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

PRESENTATIONS AND RECOGNITIONS. Ms. Marquel Sennet, Director of Communications, introduced Sa’Maya Michelle Boxley, a North Caddo High School senior, as she performed an Italian art song and Mary Did You Know. Ms. Sennet recognized Mrs. Lamesa Furlow Fleury of Eden Gardens Fundamental Magnet.  Mrs. Fleury was chosen as the 2023 Louisiana Elementary School Counselor of the Year. Then, Ms. Sennet introduced the 2023-2024 District Students of the Year for Caddo Parish Public Schools: Elementary-Bhavik Kondala of Eden Gardens Fundamental Magnet; Middle-Luka Mikaberidze of Caddo Middle Magnet; High-Analyce Jeane of North Caddo High School.  These three students will advance to the regional levels of competition. Next, Ms. Sennet introduced the 2023-2024 Support Employees of the Year: Elementary Division-Tiara Burnell, Attendance Clerk at Judson Fundamental Elementary School; Middle Division-Carlton Hardy, Security Coordinator at Caddo Middle Career & Technology School; High School Division-Charles Carey, Security Coordinator, Caddo Virtual Academy. Ms. Sennet recognized Mrs. LaToria Stewart, Principal of Broadmoor STEM Academy, as the Shreveport Chamber of Commerce chose her as a 40 Under 40 Professional. Also, the talented 3rd graders at Southern Hills Elementary School placed 1st place in the 34th Annual Greening of the Louisiana State Exhibit Museum as they decorated a Christmas Tree with ornaments constructed in art class. They received a $150 price for first place. In conclusion, the first place winners from Caddo Parish in the competition Christmas in Roseland-Christmas Cards to the Community were: Middle School 1st place was Caddo Middle Magnet; High School 1st place was Huntington High School; Best in Show was Huntington High School; The People’s Choice Award Winner was Huntington High School.  Congratulations to all who were recognized tonight. 

 

VISITORS The following visitors addressed the Board on matters of interest/concern: Jon Glover, Karen White, Martha Glover, Irma Rogers, Pastor Timothy Jones, Henry Pennington, Jr., Paige Hoffpauir, Annalee Yard,  Noah Wagstaff, Tina Weindel, Jacqueline Miller, Leslye Gilchrist, Don Tinlsey, Emma Shepherd, Mr. Lucas and Ms. Goodwin of the Shreveport Bossier Astronomical Society, Jackie Lansdale, Jordan Thomas.

 

ESTABLISH THE AGENDA AND PROPOSED CONSENT AGENDA Superintendent Goree highlighted items for the Board’s consideration.  President Vinson announced the following items are on consent: 6.01-6.04, 8.03-8.04, 8.06-8.09, and 13.01.

 

Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to establish the agenda and consent agenda for the December 19, 2023, CPSB meeting as presented. The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

CONFIRM THE CONSENT AGENDA Mr. Dixon moved, seconded by Mr. Little, to confirm the consent agenda for the December 19, 2023, CPSB Meeting.  The vote on the motion carried, and the following is a summary of the Board’s action on the consent agenda items.

 

6.01 Personnel Recommendations.  The Board approved the Personnel Recommendations as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

6.02 Personnel Leaves.  The Board approved the Personnel Leaves as recommended by the staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

6.03 Personnel Resignations.  The Board approved the Personnel Resignations as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

6.04 Administrative Contract Renewals.  The Board approved the Administrative Contract Renewals as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.03 Consideration to approve revisions to CPSB Policy GAMC. The Board approved the revisions to CPSB Policy GAMC as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.04 Consideration to approve 2023-2024 DOEC Interagency Agreements.  The Board approved the 2023-2024 Interagency Agreements as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.06 Consideration to approve a one-time Differentiated Compensation for Critical Shortage Areas. The Board approved a one-time Differentiated Compensation for Critical Shortage Areas as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.07 Consideration to approve amendment to CPSB Policy ED.  The Board approved an amendment to CPSB Policy ED as recommended by Board Member Steve Umling and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.08 Consideration to begin the process to change the name of Northside Middle School to Linear/Northside Middle School. The Board approved to begin the process to change the name of Northside Middle School to Linear/Northside Middle School as recommended by Board Member Jasmine Green and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.09 Consideration to begin the process to rename Woodlawn Leadership Academy back to Woodlawn High School.  The Board approved to begin the process to rename Woodlawn Leadership Academy back to Woodlawn High School as recommended by Board Member Mary Trammel and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

13.01 Student Readmission Appeal Hearings.  The Board approved staff recommendations for students CC, MM, and KA as parents are in agreement and submitted in the electronic mailout.

                                                                                                                                               

7.01 Capital Project Bids. After discussion, Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Mr. Little, to approve the bid as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout on the bid tabulation sheets: Project 2025-245 “North Caddo High Turf Football Field,” be awarded to Hellas Construction (Not OCC*), Cedar Park Texas, with a Base Bid and Alternate 1, for the sum total of $2,516,323.00.  The budget amount is $2,800,000.00.  The project will be paid from Capital Project Funds. The vote on the motion carried, with Mrs. McLain opposed.

 

7.02 Purchasing Bids.  After discussion, Mrs. Tharpe moved, seconded by Mr. Dixon, to reject the Bid for the 03L-24-Lease-Worley Observatory Property awarded to Shreveport-Bossier Astronomical Society, Inc. The vote on the motion carried, with Mrs. Bell opposed.  Then, Mrs. Tharpe moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to approve the following bids as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout on the scoring tabulation sheet (1) RFP 02-24-Network Electronics awarded to Presidio Networked Solutions, LLC (2) RFP 03-24-Internet Access Service NOC awarded to Conterra Ultra Broadband, LLC (3) RFP 04-24-Internet Access Service SNOC awarded to Conterra Ultra Broadband, LLC (4) RFP 05-24 – Student Uniforms and Accessories awarded to Shreveport Gymnastic Supply Co., Inc. (5) 02-24 – Agricultural Lease – Herndon Property awarded to Chad Peninger. The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

8.02  Consideration of a Resolution recognizing the final form and execution of the Bond Purchase Agreement in connection with the issuance and sale of $8,500,000 General Obligation School Bonds, Series 2024, of the Parishwide school district of Caddo Parish Louisiana, and providing for other matters in connection therewith. After discussion, Mr. Little moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to approve a Resolution recognizing the final form and execution of the Bond Purchase Agreement in connection with the issuance and sale of $8,500,000 General Obligation School Bonds, Series 2024, of the Parishwide school district of Caddo Parish Louisiana, and providing for other matters in connection therewith as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.  The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

8.05 Consideration to Approve the Proposed 2024-2025 School Year Calendar.  After discussion, Mrs. McLain moved, seconded by Mr. Dixon, to approve the proposed 2024-2025 School Year Calendar as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout. The vote on the motion carried, with Mr. Umling opposed. 

 

8.10 Consideration to approve the recommended changes to the Caddo Parish School Board Health, Dental, Life, and Disability Plans.  After a lengthy discussion, (1) a substitute motion was made by Mrs. McLain and seconded by Ms. Trammel to approve an increase in premiums as recommended by staff but allow the deductibles and out-of-pocket expenses to remain at current levels.  (2) Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mr. Little, to call for the question to end debate.  The vote on the motion to call for the question failed, with Ms. Green, Dr. Vinson, Mr. Dixon, Mrs. McLain, and Mrs Yeates opposed. (3) Ms. Green moved, seconded by Dr. Douzart, to postpone the vote on Agenda Item 8.10 until the next board meeting. After a lengthy discussion (4) Mrs. Tharpe moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel to call for the question to end debate  The vote on the motion to end debate failed, with Ms. Green, Dr. Vinson, Dr. Douzart, Mrs McLain, and Mrs. Bell opposed.  The discussion to postpone the vote continued, and the board voted. (5) The vote on the motion to postpone the vote until the next board meeting carried with Mr. Little, Ms. Trammel, Mrs. Tharpe, and Mrs. McLain opposed. Then, the Board President called for a 10-minute break.

 

8.11 Election of 2024 CPSB Officers

President – Jessica Yeates (Yes: Umling, Green, Vinson, Little, Trammel, Dixon, Tharpe, Rachal, McLain, Yeates, and Bell  Abstained: Douzart); Vice President – Dottie Bell (Yes: Umling, Green, Vinson, Little, Trammel, Dixon, Tharpe, Rachal, McLain, Yeates, Bell  No: Douzart) 2nd Vice President – Barry Rachal (Yes: Green, Trammel, Dixon, Tharpe, Rachal, McLain, Yeates, Bell   No: Umling, Little  Abstained: Vinson, Douzart)

 

8.12 Approval of President’s Use of Facsimile Signature Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mrs. Bell, to approve the President’s use of Facsimile Signature as recommended by staff.  The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

13.02 EXECUTIVE  SESSION.  Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Dr. Douzart, to move into Executive Session for the purpose of the Superintendent’s Evaluation and Review of the Contract and Goals.  The vote on the motion carried, and the Board went into Executive Session.

The Board reconvened in open session.

Mrs. Yeates moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to approve the Superintendent’s evaluation.  The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

Mrs. Yeates moved, seconded by Mr.Rachal, to authorize the Board President to evaluate whether the Superintendent is entitled to incentive pay based on his achievements relative to the Board approved goals. The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

8.13 Consideration of Extension of Superintendent’s Contract  Mrs. Bell moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to extend the Superintendent’s contract for four years, maintaining all terms as currently defined with the contract with one exception noted in that it be amended to allow for receipt of any future state or local salary increases received by any other certificated employee of the system.  The vote on the motion carried, with Dr. Vinson opposed and Ms. Green not present at vote.

 

ADJOURNMENT. Mrs.Bell moved, seconded by Mrs.Yeates, to adjourn.  The vote on the motion carried, with Ms. Green not present at vote. The December 19, 2023, CPSB Meeting adjourned at approximately 10:45 p.m.
