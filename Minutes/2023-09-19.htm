September 19, 2023

CPSB Meeting

 

Members presentSteve Umling, Jasmine Green, Dr. Terence Vinson, Don Little,  Dr. Bonita Douzart, Mary Trammel, Darrin Dixon, Christine Tharpe, Barry Rachal, Katie McLain, Jessica Yeates, and Dottie Bell. Also present were Dr. T. Lamar Goree, Secretary, and Mr. Reggie Abrams, Board Counsel.

 

President Dr. Terence Vinson called the September 19, 2023, meeting to order, and Board Member Barry Rachal gave the Invocation and led the Pledge of Allegiance.

 

CONSIDERATION OF THE MINUTES OF THE AUGUST 1, 2023, and AUGUST 15, 2023, CPSB MEETINGS. Mr. Dixon moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to approve the minutes of the August 1, 2023, and August 15, 2023, CPSB meetings as submitted in the electronic mailout.  Vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

PRESENTATIONS AND RECOGNITIONS.

Ms. Marquel Sennett, Director of Communications, introduced Jeremiah White, a talented and gifted senior at Southwood High School, as he performed “Ribbon in the Sky.” Jeremiah was accompanied by his vocal specialist, Mr. Scott Garrett. Ms. Sennett introduced ten students who have joined the ranks of Caddo Parish Public School’s Perfect ACT Club.

 

VISITORS

The following visitors addressed the Board on matters of interest/concern:  Jordan Thomas, Red River United, voiced concerns about the salary schedule, employee shortage, and the changes with Blue Cross/Blue Shield.

 

ESTABLISH THE AGENDA AND PROPOSED CONSENT AGENDA

Superintendent Goree highlighted items for the Board’s consideration.  President Vinson announced the following items are on consent: 6.02-6.04, 8.02-8.04, 8.06-8.08, 8.11-8.13, and 13.01

 

Mr. Little moved, seconded by Mr. Umling, to establish the agenda and consent agenda for the September 19, 2023, CPSB meeting as presented.  Mrs. Bell moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to add an agenda item to approve of Resolution authorizing the retention of special counsel for representation in the Social Media Litigation and authorizing the execution of an attorney-client fee contract with Ron Austin of Ron Austin Law. After discussion, Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Ms. Green, to call for the question and end the debate.  1) The vote on the motion carried, and the debate ended with Mr. Little and Mrs. McLain opposed. 2) The vote on the motion to add the item to the agenda failed, being it must be a unanimous vote, with Mr. Little and Mr. Umling opposed.  3) The vote on the original motion carried to establish the agenda and consent agenda for the September 19, 2023, CPSB meeting.

 

CONFIRM THE CONSENT AGENDA

Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mr. Dixon, to confirm the consent agenda for the September 19, 2023, CPSB Meeting.  Vote on the motion carried, and the following is a summary of the Board’s action on the consent agenda items.

 

6.02 Personnel Leaves.  The Board approved the Personnel Leaves as recommended by the Superintendent and submitted in the electronic mailout

 

6.03 Personnel Resignations.  The Board approved the Personnel Resignations as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

6.04 Administrative Contract Renewals.  The Board approved the Administrative Contract Renewals as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.02 Consideration to authorize salvage sale. The Board approved salvage sale as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.03 Consideration to approve Louisiana Compliance Questionnaire. The Board approved the Louisiana Compliance Questionnaire as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.04 Consideration to approve revisions to the 2023-2024 Caddo Personnel Evaluation Plan. The Board approved revisions to 2023-2024 Caddo Personnel Evaluation Plan as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.06 Consideration to approve Resolution and Extract of Resolution for the sale of the J.B. Harville Property-Land Only. The Board approved the Resolution and Extract of Resolution for the sale of the J.B. Harville Property-Land only as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.07 Consideration to approve Resolution and Extract of Resolution for the sale of the Barret Paideia Academy Property.  The Board approved the Resolution and Extract of Resolution for the sale of the Barret Paideia Academy as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.08 Consideration to Approve Resolution and Extract of Resolution for the sale of the one-acre tract of land on Hale School Road, Hosston, LA.  The Board approved the Resolution and Extract of Resolution for the sale of the one-acre tract of land on Hale School Road, Hosston, LA as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.11 Consideration to approve revision to Policy FDC – Naming of Facilities. The Board approved a revision to Policy FDC-Naming of Facilities as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.12 Consideration to approve new CPSB Policy INB.  The Board approved a new CPSB Policy INB as recommended by staff and submitted it in the electronic mailout.

 

8.13 Consideration to approve DOEC 2023-2024 Interagency Agreements and Contracts.  The Board approved DOEC 2023-2024 Interagency Agreements and Contracts as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

13.01 Student Readmission Appeal Hearings.  The Board approved staff recommendations for students JH, JD, and MD as parents are in agreement as submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.05 Consideration to approve ITEP property tax exemption for Allen’s Electric Motor Service After discussion, Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Mrs. Bell, to approve ITEP property tax exemption for Allen’s Electric Motor Service as submitted in the electronic mailout. The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

8.09 Approval of Salary Schedule.  After discussion, Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to continue to approve the salary schedule as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout. The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

8.10 Consideration to approve pay increase for Substitute ISS Aides and Substitute Bus Attendants to $10.00 per hour.  After discussion, Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to approve a pay increase for substitute ISS Aides and Substitute Bus Attendants to $10.00 per hour. The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

8.14 Consideration to approve Second Supplemental Capital Projects and Professional Designers for 2023-2024 SY. After discussion, Mr. Umling moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to approve second supplemental Capital Projects and Professional Designers for 2023-2024 SY as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.  The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

ANNOUNCEMENTS. Ms. Green said this year’s Soul Bowl was outstanding, and Green Oaks and BTW should be very proud. She told Dr. Vinson they should consider making this a citywide event. Mr. Little thanked Mr. Roberts and Mr. Greer for the well-done presentation at the Transportation Committee Meeting.  He also thanked staff, particularly Mr. Greer and Mr. White, for joining him in the exploration of Sears, and he hopes this makes it to the Building and Grounds Committee in due time. Mr. Rachal requested a report on the potential sale of Blue Cross/Shield and requested procedures for TAT Teachers. Ms. Trammel announced that today, CMM received the National Blue Ribbon Award. Dr. Goree stated this is the third time Caddo Middle Magnet has received the National Blue Ribbon Award.  He added that CMM is one of nine schools in the State of Louisiana to receive the award, which is the highest award that a school can receive from the Department of Education.  Also, it is the only middle school in the State of Louisiana and one of twenty-nine schools in the country to receive the award. Dr. Douzart said Thursday that the Honorable Mayor announced that Grambling State University and Southern University was the biggest Bayou Classic in the United States. She added, “Grambling is in the south, Grambling is in the north, Grambling is in the east, and Grambling is in the west, and Grambling is the best.” Mr. Dixon said the Soul Bowl had lots of energy and complimented BTW Band as one of the area's best.  He added he is donating his trombone to BTW Band and buying them a trumpet.  He challenged his colleagues and the community to help the bands as they grow.

 

EXECUTIVE  SESSION. 

13.02 Student Expulsion Appeal Hearing. Mrs. Yeates moved, seconded by Mrs. McLain, to move into Executive Session for the purpose of hearing the Student Expulsion Appeal Hearing.  The vote on the motion carried with Ms. Green and Mrs. Tharpe not present at vote, and the Board went into executive session at approximately 6:25 p.m. The Board reconvened in open session at approximately 7:21 p.m. Mr. Little moved, seconded by Mrs. McLain, to uphold the expulsion of student RY as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout. The vote on the motion carried, with Mr. Umling and Dr. Vinson opposed, and Ms. Green and Mrs. Tharpe not present at vote.

 

13.03 Oral Argument and Disposition of Grievance of JD. Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to move into Executive Session for the purpose of hearing the oral argument and disposition of Level IV Grievance of employee JD.  The vote on the motion carried with Ms. Green and Mrs. Tharpe not present at the vote, and the Board went into executive session at approximately 7:26 p.m. The Board reconvened in open session at approximately 8:10 p.m. Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mr. Dixon, to uphold staff’s recommendation to dispose of the grievance of JD.  The vote on the motion carried with Mr. Umling, Dr. Vinson, Dr.Douzart,  Ms. Trammel, Mr. Dixon, Mr. Rachal, Mrs. McLain, Mrs.Yeates, and Mrs. Bell supporting the motion, Mr. Little opposed, and Ms. Green and Mrs.Tharpe not present at vote.

 

13.04 Oral Argument and Consideration of Employee Termination Appeal in Accordance with CPSB Policy GBN and Policy GAE.  Mrs. Yeates moved, seconded by Mrs. Trammel, to move into Executive Session for the purpose of hearing the oral argument and consideration of employee termination appeal in accordance with CPSB Policy GBN and Policy GAE.  The vote on the motion carried with Ms. Green and Mrs. Tharpe not present at vote, and the Board went into executive session at approximately 8:14 p.m. The Board reconvened in open session.

Mr. Dixon moved, seconded by Mrs. Bell, to extend unpaid leave to November 23, 2023, at which time the employee shall return to work with a doctor’s release otherwise, she is terminated immediately.

A substitute motion was made by Mr. Rachal, seconded by Mr. Little, to uphold the termination.  The vote on the motion failed, with Dr. Vinson, Dr. Douzart, Mr. Dixon, Mrs. Yeates, and Mrs. Bell opposed, and Ms. Green and Mrs. Tharpe not present at vote.

The board then voted on the original motion made by Mr. Dixon, seconded by Mrs. Bell,  to extend unpaid leave to November 23, 2023, at which time the employee shall return to work with a doctor’s release otherwise, she is terminated immediately.  The vote on the motion failed with Mr. Umling, Mr. Little, Ms. Trammel, Mr. Rachal, and Mrs. McLain opposed and Ms. Green and Mrs. Tharpe not present at vote.

Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Mr. Little, to uphold the termination.  The vote on the motion carried, with Dr. Vinson, Mr. Dixon, and Mrs. Bell opposed and Ms. Green and Mrs. Tharpe not present at vote.

 

ADJOURNMENT. Mr. Umling moved, seconded by Mrs.Yeates, to adjourn.  The vote on the motion carried, and the September 19, 2023, CPSB Meeting adjourned at approximately

9:25 p.m.
