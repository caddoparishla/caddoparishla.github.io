INSTRUCTIONAL NEUTRALITY

 

 

The Caddo Parish School System is committed to providing an educational environment that prioritizes learning, critical thinking, and respect for diverse viewpoints.  To ensure that the academic environment remains focused on education and free from political influence, the Caddo Parish School Board adopts this policy of instructional neutrality.  This policy aligns with federal and state law, fostering an inclusive and impartial educational environment for all students.

 

The Caddo Parish School Board acknowledges that diverse viewpoints are vital to a thriving society and essential for fostering critical thinking in students.  This policy does not infringe upon constitutionally protected expression but ensures that classrooms remain spaces where all perspectives can be discussed respectfully and without undue influence.

 

PROHIBITED POLITICAL DISPLAYS

 

Teachers, staff, and administrators are prohibited from wearing or displaying politically sensitive clothing, pins, badges, or symbols.  This includes, but is not limited to, items advocating for political parties, candidates, ideologies, or political groups that take positions on race, gender, sexuality, or sexual identity or any other protected characteristic.

 

POLITICAL NEUTRALITY IN THE CLASSROOM

 

Employees are prohibited from using the classroom or their official positions to promote personal political beliefs, parties, or causes.  Classrooms must remain free of partisan advocacy or endorsement of ideologies that could influence or coerce students.  Teaching must be rooted in state standards and guided by evidence-based practices and approved curricula.

 

FOSTERING DISCUSSION AND DEBATE

 

Students are encouraged to engage in open discussions, debates, and critical thinking on political, social, and economic issues.  Teachers are expected to facilitate these discussions impartially, fostering respect for diverse perspectives and encouraging intellectual inquiry.  The objective is to equip students with the skills to analyze and understand differing viewpoints in an informed and respectful manner.

 

The School Board assigns the Superintendent or his/her designee responsible for implementing the administrative regulations necessary to ensure this policy is effectively applied across all schools.  These regulations shall support teachers in fostering open, balanced discussions while maintaining a neutral and inclusive environment in alignment with this policy.

 

New policy:  January 21, 2025
