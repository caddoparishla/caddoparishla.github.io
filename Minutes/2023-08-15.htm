August 15, 2023

CPSB Meeting

 

Members presentSteve Umling, Jasmine Green, Dr. Terence Vinson, Don Little,  Dr. Bonita Douzart, Mary Trammel, Darrin Dixon, Christine Tharpe, Barry Rachal, and Dottie Bell. Also present were Dr. T. Lamar Goree, Secretary, and Mr. Reggie Abrams, Board Counsel.

 

President Dr. Terence Vinson called the August 15, 2023, meeting to order, and Board Member Christine Tharpe gave the Invocation and led the Pledge of Allegiance.

 

CONSIDERATION OF THE MINUTES OF THE JULY 12, 2023, JULY 18, 2023, and JULY 20, 2023, CPSB MEETINGS. Mr. Dixon moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to approve the minutes of the July 12, 2023, July 18, 2023, and July 20, 2023, CPSB meetings as submitted in the electronic mailout.  Vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

PRESENTATIONS AND RECOGNITIONS. Ms. Marquel Sennet, Director of Communications, introduced the following recognitions for the district. Caddo Parish has the distinct pleasure of having four students on the 2023-2024 Louisiana Legislative Youth Advisory Council - Riley Quinlan and Kate Shaw of C. E. Byrd High School, Vivienne Webb of Caddo Virtual Academy, and Analyce Jeane of North Caddo Magnet High School.

 

Jatarian Montgomery, a Junior at Huntington High School, represented Caddo as a National Title Competitor at the 2nd Annual National Career Development Conference in Orlando, FL.  He was also elected as the 2023 JAG State President for Louisiana.

 

Caddo also had three more students representing the district on the state level for the JAG Program.  Katie Stevenson of Woodlawn Leadership Academy received State Recognition.  Taylor Williams of Huntington High School was elected JAG State Vice President of Career Development, and Kaden Harris of Huntington High School was elected State Secretary.  Both of these students served as National Voting Delegates. 

 

VISITORS

The following visitors addressed the Board on matters of interest/concern:  David Bellar addressed the Board regarding overturned student expulsions;  Demetria Shelton addressed the Board regarding the lack of accommodations for her disabled son; Jon Glover expressed her concern with the lack of transcription of visitor comments in the minutes; Jordan Thomas addressed the Board regarding the hiring of TAT teachers and Certified Teachers;  Dr. Pinkney asked staff to employ more new teacher coaches to assist teachers with the Praxis, etc.

 

ESTABLISH THE AGENDA AND PROPOSED CONSENT AGENDA

Superintendent Goree highlighted items for the Board’s consideration.  President Vinson announced the following items are on consent: 6.02-6.04, 7.01, 8.02-8.05, and 8.08-8.10.

 

Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to establish the agenda and consent agenda for the August 15, 2023, CPSB meeting as presented.  Vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

CONFIRM THE CONSENT AGENDA

Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Mr. Dixon, to confirm the consent agenda for the August 15, 2023, CPSB Meeting.  Vote on the motion carried, and the following is a summary of the Board’s action on the consent agenda items.

 

6.02 Personnel Leaves.  The Board approved the Personnel Leaves as recommended by the Superintendent and submitted in the electronic mailout

 

6.03 Personnel Resignations.  The Board approved the Personnel Resignations as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

6.04 Administrative Contract Renewals.  The Board approved the Administrative Contract Renewals as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

7.01 Consideration to Approve Capital Project Bids. The Board approved the bids as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout on the bid tabulation sheets: (1) "Woodlawn HVAC, Phase 2", Project 2025-801, be awarded to The Payne Company (Not OCC*), Shreveport, LA with a Base Bid, Alternate 1 and Alternate 2, for the sum total of $2,087,000.00. The budget amount is $2,000,000.00. The project will be paid from ESSER Funds. (2) "Caddo Career Technology Center HVAC, Phase 2", Project 2025-752, be awarded to FitzGerald Contractors (Not OCC*), Shreveport, LA, with a Base Bid, Alternate 1 and Alternate 2, for the sum total of $1,021,600.00. The budget amount is $1,500,000.00. The project will be paid from BOND Funds (3) "Southwood HVAC, Phase 3", Project 2025-750, be awarded to The Payne Company (Not OCC*), Shreveport, LA with a Base Bid, Alternate 1, Alternate 2 and Alternate 3, for the sum total of $1,666.000.00. The budget amount is $1,750,000.00. The project will be paid from BOND Funds (4) "Broadmoor HVAC, Phase 2", Project 2025-802, be awarded to Reliance Mechanical (Not OCC*), Shreveport, LA, with a Base Bid and Alternate 1, for the sum total of $601,600.00. The budget amount is $1,500,000.00. The project will be paid from ESSER Funds (5) "Oak Park HVAC, Phase 2", Project 2025-803, be awarded to The Payne Company (Not OCC*), Shreveport, LA, with a Base Bid, for the sum total of $1,062,000.00. The budget amount is $1,500,000.00. The project will be paid from ESSER Funds.

 

8.02 Consideration to approve 2023 -2024 General Fund, Child Nutrition Fund, and ESSER Fund Budget Revisions. The Board approved 2023-2024 General Fund, Child Nutrition Fund, and ESSER Fund Budget Revisions as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.03 Consideration to approve modifications to the 2023-2024 Board Meeting Schedule. The Board approved modifications to 2023-2024 Board Meeting Schedule as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.04 Approval of Mental Health Interagency Agreements and Contracts. The Board approved Mental Health Interagency Agreements and Contracts as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.05 Consideration to Approve Cooperative Endeavor Agreement and Lease Agreement between the City of Shreveport and Caddo Parish School Board regarding Valencia Park Softball Field. The Board approved the Cooperative Endeavor Agreement and Lease Agreement between the City of Shreveport and Caddo Parish School Board regarding Valencia Park Softball Field as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.08 Consideration to reduce the listing price of the following CPSB Properties: 79th Street (Land) from $42,525 to $39,690 and Oil City Elementary/Middle School Site from $663,000 to $464,000. The Board approved to reduce the listing price of the following CPSB Properties: 79th Street (Land) from $42,525 to $39,690 and Oil City Elementary/Middle Site from $663,000 to $464,000 as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.09 Consideration to list the one-acre tract of land adjacent to Paradise Baptist Church for sale at the appraised value.  The Board approved to list the one-acre tract of land adjacent to Paradise Baptist Church for sale at the appraised value as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.10 Consideration to Approve the Distribution Plan for the One-Time Stipend to Employees.  The Board approved the Distribution Plan for the one-time stipend to employees as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.06 Disposition of Grievance of Julie Dickinson After discussion, Ms. Green moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to set matter on the agenda for oral argument at the next board meeting in accordance with Policy GAE. The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

8.07 Consideration to continue to negotiate terms of the purchase offer of $425,000 for Barrett Paidea Academy from MGM Development Group, LLC.  After discussion, Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to continue to negotiate terms of the purchase offer of $425,000 for Barrett Paidea Academy from MGM Development Group, LLC, as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout. The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

8.11 Consideration of Employee Termination Appeal in Accordance with CPSB Policy GBN and Policy GAE.  After discussion, Mr. Dixon moved, seconded by Mrs. Bell, to set matter on the agenda for oral argument at the next board meeting in accordance with Policy GAE. The vote on the motion carried, with Mrs. Tharpe opposed.
