May 23, 2023

CPSB Meeting

 

Members present Steve Umling, Jasmine Green, Dr. Terence Vinson, Don Little, Dr. Bonita Douzart, Mary Trammel, Barbara Smith-Iverson, Christine Tharpe, Barry Rachal, Katie McLain, Jessica Yeates, and Dottie Bell. Also present were Superintendent Dr. T. Lamar Goree and Mr. Reggie Abrams, Board Counsel.

 

President Dr. Terence Vinson called the May 23, 2023, CPSB meeting to order, and Dr. Bonita Douzart gave the Invocation and led the Pledge of Allegiance.

 

Consideration of Minutes of the April 18, 2023, and May 23, 2023, CPSB Meetings. Mrs. Bell moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to approve the minutes of the April 18, 2023, May 23, 2023, and April 4, 2023, CPSB meetings as submitted in the electronic mailout.  The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

PRESENTATIONS AND RECOGNITIONS

Ms. Marquel Sennet, Director of Communications, recognized the AmeriCorps Seniors (SUSLA) Caddo/Bossier Desoto Foster Grandparents.  She explained that the program allows seniors 55 and older to serve as mentors, tutors, and loving caregivers for youth. Ms. Sennet added that the program continues to serve the community after 43 years, and the district thanks the volunteers and the Director, Ms. Neva Jones. Next, Ms. Sennet introduced Mr. Lyndon B. Johnson, Calumet Community and Government Relations Specialist for Northwest Louisiana, as he recognized five students who received medallions at the Science Fair Award Ceremony on March 11. Mr. Johnson announced that for the second year, Calumet Specialty Products Partners, L.P., would award local students a total of $5,000.00 in scholarships in support of STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Mathematics) Education – Tessa Atkins and Gage Smith, both students at Eden Gardens Magnet School and Jack Randall, Caddo Magnet High School.  Ms. Sennet announced that Caddo’s Raj Letchuman of Caddo Parish Magnet High School was selected as the 2023 High School Louisiana Student of the Year. Raj earned a perfect ACT score, and serves his community through tutoring and volunteering.  He plans to attend Yale University with a career goal of becoming an astronaut while accepting an internship with NASA. On June 15, 2023, Raj will be presented to the LA BESE, where they annually honor the top elementary, middle, and high school students in the State.  Ms. Sennet stated that the Caddo School Counselor’s Association selected three outstanding educators as the 2022-2023 Elementary, Middle, and High School Counselors of the Year – Mrs. Veronica Dougles-Johnson of University Elementary, Mrs. Veronica Trahan of Caddo Middle Career and Technology and Ms. Pamela Radford of Woodlawn Leadership Academy. Congratulations to Principal Christopher Jackson, staff, students, and families of Midway Professional Development Center for being recognized as one of the ELA Louisiana Comeback Campuses.  Midway increased the percentage of students scoring mastery and above and decreased the percentage of students scoring unsatisfactory in ELA on statewide assessments compared to pre-pandemic in 2019.  Creswell Elementary and Green Oaks Performing Arts Academy have been recognized as Math Louisiana Comeback Campuses.  These schools increased the percentage of students scoring mastery and above and decreased the percentage of students scoring unsatisfactory in Math.  Congratulations to Principal Alton Dozier of Creswell Elementary and Principal Steven Grant of Green Oaks Performing Arts. Ms. Sennet recognized Sophie Chen, a Senior at Caddo Magnet High School.  Sophie is one of 62 students chosen for the summer science and engineering program at the Research Science Institute at Massachusetts Institute of Technology Research Science Institute. This is a cost-free five-week research internship where Sophie will conduct individual projects with experienced scientists and researchers as mentors, and in the final week, she will prepare her written and oral presentations.

ESTABLISH THE AGENDA AND PROPOSED CONSENT AGENDA

Superintendent Goree highlighted items for the Board’s consideration.  President announced the following items are on consent: 6.01-6.03, and 8.01-8.04

 

Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to establish the agenda and consent agenda for the May 23, 2023, CPSB meeting as presented.  The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

CONFIRM THE CONSENT AGENDA

Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Dr. Douzart, to confirm the consent agenda for the May 23, 2023, CPSB Meeting.  The vote on the motion carried, and the following is a summary of the Board’s action on the consent agenda items.

 

6.01 Personnel Recommendation.  The Board approved the Personnel Recommendation as recommended by the Superintendent and submitted in the electronic mailout.

6.02 Personnel Leaves.  The Board approved the Personnel Leaves as recommended by the Superintendent and submitted in the electronic mailout

6.03 Personnel Resignations.  The Board approved the Personnel Resignations as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.01 Property/Bus Rental Requests. The Board approved the property/bus rental requests as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.02 Consideration of Approval of Caddo-Shreveport Sales and Use Tax Commission 2023-2024 Budgets. The Board approved the Caddo-Shreveport Sales and Use Tax Commission 2023-2024 Budgets as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.03 Consideration to approve ratification, modification, and extension of the Lease Agreement between the Caddo Parish School Board and the Parish of Caddo in relation to the Public Park and Recreational Area located on the Keithville Elementary/Middle School Property. The Board approved the ratification, modification, and extension of the Lease Agreement between the Caddo Parish School Board and the Parish of Caddo in relation to the Public Park and Recreational Area located on the Keithville Elementary/Middle School Property as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.04 Consideration to Authorize Salvage Sale. The Board approved the authorization of the salvage sale of general classroom items and supplies in accordance with School Board policy as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

Consideration to approve Summer 2024 ESSER and Bond Funds Projects Plan with Designers. Mrs. Bell moved, seconded by Steve Umling, to approve the Summer 2024 ESSER and Bond Funds Projets Plan with Designers as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout. After a lengthy discussion, Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Ms. Green, to call for the question to end the debate.  The vote on the motion carried unanimously, and the discussion ended. The Board proceeded and voted on the original motion to approve the Summer 2024 ESSER and Bond Funds Projects Plan with Designers. The vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

Consideration of changing the meeting time for Board Meetings and Executive Committee Meetings.  Mrs. Yeates moved, seconded by Mrs. McLain, to consider changing the meeting time for Board Meetings and Executive Committee Meetings to 5:00 p.m. After a lengthy discussion, Mr. Little moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to call for the question to end the discussion.  The vote carried to end the discussion, with Mr. Umling, Mr. Rachal, Mrs. McLain, and Mrs. Yeates opposed. The Board voted on the original motion to approve changing the meeting time for Board Meetings and Executive Committee Meetings to 5:00 p.m. The vote on the motion failed, with Ms. Green, Mr. Little, Dr. Douzart, Ms. Trammel, Ms. Smith-Iverson, and Mrs. Tharpe opposed.

 

Student Expulsion Appeal Hearings.  Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to move to Executive Session for 25 minutes.  The vote on the motion carried unanimously. Mrs. McLain moved, seconded by Ms. Green, to reduce JG’s expulsion to an extended suspension with a behavior contract in place for the first semester of the 2023-2024 school year. The vote on the motion carried, with Mrs. Tharpe opposed and Dr. Douzart not present at the vote. Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Mrs. Bell, to postpone the expulsion hearing for JY.  The vote on the motion carried, with Mr. Umling opposed and Dr. Douzart not present at the vote.

 

ANNOUNCEMENTS

 

ADJOURNMENT. Ms. Smith-Iverson moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to adjourn. The vote on the motion carried, with Dr. Douzart not present at the vote,  and the May 23, 2023, CPSB Meeting adjourned at approximately 8:45 p.m.
