February 21, 2023

CPSB Meeting

 

Members present Steve Umling, Jasmine Green, Dr. Terence Vinson, Don Little, Mary Trammel, Barbara Smith-Iverson, Christine Tharpe, Barry Rachal, Katie McLain, Jessica Yeates, and Dottie Bell. Also present were Dr. T. Lamar Goree, Secretary, and Mr. Reggie Abrams, Board Counsel.

 

President Dr. Terence Vinson called the meeting to order, and Board Member Jasmine Green led the Invocation and the Pledge of Allegiance.

 

CONSIDERATION OF MINUTES OF THE JANUARY 10, 2023, AND JANUARY 17, 2023, CPSB MEETINGS Dottie Bell moved, seconded by Mary Trammel, to approve the minutes of the January 10, 2023, and January 17, 2023, CPSB meetings as submitted in the electronic mailout.  Vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

PRESENTATIONS AND RECOGNITIONS Ms. Marquel Sennet, Director of Communications, introduced Northside Elementary School’s Dance and Percussion Troupe and the school’s Drumline as the audience enjoyed the unique blend of sounds and dance styles led by their music teacher, Ms. Geraldine Walker.  Ms. Sennet then recognized the 2022-2023 New Teachers of the Year: Elementary – Ms. Destiny Jackson of Werner Park Elementary, Middle – Kimberly Hache of Keithville Elementary/Middle School; High – Victoria Herbert of Huntington High School.  This group represents the first recipients to receive this award as they already demonstrate excellence in the classroom and commitment to student success, which exemplifies Louisiana’s teaching profession. Next, the 2022-2023 Teachers of the Year in Caddo were recognized: Elementary - Ms. Janet McCrevan of South Highlands Elementary School; Middle – Mrs. Sarah Kate McMillan of Caddo Middle Magnet School; and High – Mrs. Jana Panos of C. E. Byrd High School.  Congratulations to Principal Belinda Stewart, teachers, staff, students, parents, and community partners for Atkins being named a Louisiana Comeback Campus by the Louisiana Department of Education. Ms. Sennet explained that 41 schools in the state were recognized as Comeback Campuses.  These schools have made significant gains in both math and English language arts since the pandemic. Congratulations to C. E. Byrd High School senior James Frank Stratton.  He has been selected to represent the State of Louisiana in the latest cohort of the 61st Annual United States Senate Youth Program. Ms. Sennet added that this highly competitive merit-based program provides the most outstanding high school students – two from each state, and an intensive week-long study of the federal government and the people who lead it.  James will receive a $10,000 scholarship. Chloe Miller of C. E. Byrd High School and Andrew Minagar of Caddo Magnet High School were selected to join the nation’s most promising young scientists in the 2023 Regeneron Science Talent Search. Ms. Sennet said The Society for Science announced the top 300 scholars for the nation’s oldest science and math competition for high school seniors.  They will each be awarded $2000, and their schools will receive $2000. She added that the scholars were chosen based on their outstanding research, commitment to academics, creativity in asking scientific questions, and exceptional promise as STEM leaders.

 

VISITORS The following visitors addressed the Board on matters of interest/concern: Jon Glover had concerns with Agenda Item 8.03 regarding the addendum to the 2022-2023 Caddo Pupil Progression Plan;  Tiny Wyndell asked for the Board to reaccess the health insurance deductible for employees; Tammy Carey asked staff to evaluate paraprofessional wages/pay; Jessica Tullis in support of the 5:30 time change for board meetings; Robert Wright asked staff to look at flexible spending account for employees for medical and daycare expenses; Jan Smith supports the sale of the Timmons property; Kedra Bradford concerned with accommodations for her 3rd-grade student; Angela Douglas asked to file a formal complaint against Fair Park Middle School due to her son’s wrongful suspension; Orlisa Nash Johnson supports the 5:30 time change for board meetings and asks what the plans were for the old Northside Elementary building; Jordan Thomas, RRU, fully endorses the 5:30 time change for board meetings.

 

ESTABLISH THE AGENDA AND PROPOSED CONSENT AGENDA Superintendent Goree highlighted items for the Board’s consideration.  President announced the following items are on consent: 6.01-6.04,  8.01-8.09, and 13.01.

 

Mrs. Bell moved, seconded by Mr. Little, to establish the agenda and consent agenda for the February 21, 2023, CPSB meeting as presented.  Vote on the motion carried unanimously.
