April 18, 2023

CPSB Meeting

 

Members present Steve Umling, Jasmine Green, Dr. Terence Vinson, Don Little, Dr. Bonita Douzart, Mary Trammel, Barbara Smith-Iverson, Christine Tharpe, Barry Rachal, Katie McLain, Jessica Yeates, and Dottie Bell. Also present were Dr. T. Lamar Goree, Secretary, and Mr. Reggie Abrams, Board Counsel.

 

President Dr. Terence Vinson called the April 18, 2023, meeting to order, and Board Member Don Little gave the Invocation and led the Pledge of Allegiance.

 

Consideration of Minutes of the March 6, 2023, March 21, 2023, and April 4, 2023, CPSB Meetings. Mrs. Bell moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel,  to approve the minutes of the March 6, 2023, March 21, 2023, and April 4, 2023, CPSB meetings as submitted in the electronic mailout.  Vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

PRESENTATIONS AND RECOGNITIONS. Caddo’s Director of Communications, Marquel Sennet, introduced the Southwood Jazz Band under the dynamic leadership of Mr. Lennard Holden:  Jordan Black, Kaiden Green, Michael Tucker, Jaylern McKenzie, Zachary Fretwell, and Taylor Lafitte.  They entertained the crowd with Don’t Worry ‘Bout a Thing by Stevie Wonder, and I’d Rather Go Blind by Etta James. The Executive Director of Volunteers for Youth Justice, Ms. Kelly Todd, thanked the Board for all the support in making The Harbor a dream come true and presented the Board with the 2022 Heroes of the Year award. Caddo had the distinct pleasure of having six students honored as Louisiana Young Hero by Louisiana Public Broadcasting. Vivienne Webb, a Caddo Parish Magnet High School junior, is our hero. Through her testimony at the Louisiana Capitol, she advocated for and helped obtain $2 million in funding for the lowest-funded Human Service Districts.  She will be recognized on April 24 in Baton Rouge on Louisiana Young Heroes Day”. The LDOE announced its 2024 Teacher of the Year semifinalists; among those 57 teachers that made the list, Caddo’s own, Ms. Janet McCrevan from South Highlands Elementary Magnet.  She is Caddo’s Elementary Teacher of the Year and the Regional 7 winner.  She will be honored at the 17th Annual Cecil J. Picard Educator Excellence Awards Gala in New Orleans on July 22nd.

 

VISITORS The following visitors addressed the Board on matters of interest/concern:  Lola Lawson, Shreveport was concerned with the reconfiguration of Green Oaks; Otis Williams, Shreveport, LA  was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Sandy Boykin, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Eleanor Collins, Shreveport, LA opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Dr. Mary Nash Robinson, Greenwood, LA spoke on the closing of Stoner Hill and expressed she understood the hard decisions the board must make; Sinora Harris, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Jordan Barron, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Shakavisos Goff, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Sarah Rice, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Sierra Linette, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Pastor Don Tayler of Stoner Hill Baptist Church was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Shannon Amidon, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Breka Peoples, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Omari Ho-Sang, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Twanna White, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Marvin Muhammad, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Latitia Burgh, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Bernard Friday, Red River United, spoke on the subject of Teacher Retention; Rachal Jackson, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Willie Bradford, Shreveport, LA was opposed to the closing of Stoner Hill; Jon Glover, Shreveport, LA voiced her concern regarding Agenda Items 8.02 and 8.05.

 

ESTABLISH THE AGENDA AND PROPOSED CONSENT AGENDA Superintendent Goree highlighted items for the Board’s consideration.  President announced the following items are on consent: 6.01-6.03, 7.01, 8.01, 8.03-8.04, 8.06, and 13.02.

 

Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to establish the agenda and consent agenda for the April 18, 2023, CPSB meeting as presented.  Vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

CONFIRM THE CONSENT AGENDA Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to confirm the consent agenda for the April 18, 2023, CPSB Meeting.  Vote on the motion carried, and the following is a summary of the Board’s action on the consent agenda items.

 

6.01 Personnel Recommendations.  The Board approved the Personnel Recommendations as recommended by the Superintendent and submitted in the electronic mailout.

6.02 Personnel Leaves.  The Board approved the Personnel Leaves as recommended by the Superintendent and submitted in the electronic mailout.

6.03 Personnel Resignations.  The Board approved the Personnel Resignations as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

7.01 Consideration to Approve Capital Project Bids.The Board approved the bids as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout on the bid tabulation sheet:  1) Project 2024-231, "Creswell Structural, Phase 2", be awarded to Thomas & Parker Waterproofing (Not OCC*), Shreveport, LA, with a Base Bid and Alternate 1, for the sum total of $828,040.00 The budget amount is $1,000,000.00. The project will be paid from Capital Projects Funds. 2) Project 2024-229, "Summer Grove Roofing,” be awarded to Johnny's Roofing Metal Works (Not OCC*), Shreveport, LA, with a Base Bid, Alternate 1 and Alternate 2, for the sum total of $154,370.00. The budget amount is $300,000.00.The project will be paid from Capital Projects Funds.

 

8.01 Property/Bus Rental Requests. The Board approved the property/bus rental requests as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout. 

 

8.03 Consideration to modify the Board meeting schedule to host the May Regular Session meeting on May 23, 2023.  The Board approved the modification of the Board Meeting schedule to host the May Regular Session meeting on May 23, 2023, as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.04 Adoption of 2023 Millage Rates.  The Board approved a resolution adopting millage rates to be levied on taxable property in Caddo Parish for 2023, with the total recommended levy being 74.77 mills, as submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.06 Consideration of Authorization of Caddo Parish School Board to Participate in Jansen Opioid Settlement. The Board approved to authorize the Caddo Parish School Board to participate in Jansen Opioid Settlement as submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

13.02 Request for Settlement Authority – Claimant: Jamie Hill – Date of Loss 5/24/22 – Claim #4A2205VFJC8-00001. The Board approved the settlement as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

10-minute recess was taken.

 

8.02 Consideration to approve a contract renewal with AMIKids-Caddo, Inc. to continue operating as the Alternative School Service Provider for grades 6-8 without their Charter School designation.  Mrs. Bell moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to approve the proposed renewal agreement, which allows AMIKids-Caddo, Inc. to continue operating as our Alternative Service Provider for grades 6-8, without their Charter School designation, in Caddo Parish Public Schools as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.  Motion carried.  Dr. Douzart and Mrs. Smith-Iverson abstained.  Ms. Green was not present at the vote.

 

8.05 Consideration to consolidate the Attendance Zones of E. B. Williams Stoner Hill Elementary School and Creswell Elementary School and the Special Education Classes to transfer as units beginning with the 2023-2024 school year. After a lengthy discussion, Mr. Little moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to call for the question to end the debate. The motion failed to have a 2/3 majority vote, with Dr. Vinson, Mr. Rachal, Mrs. McLain, and Mrs. Yeates opposed and Ms. Green not present at the vote. The discussion resumed. Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to call for the question to end the debate. The motion carried with Dr. Vinson, Mrs. McLain, and Mrs. Yeates opposed, and Ms. Green was not present at the vote. Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Mrs. Bell, to approve the consolidation of the attendance zones of E. B. Williams Stoner Hill Elementary School with Creswell Elementary School and the Special Education Classes to transfer as units beginning with the 2023-2024 school year as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout. Motion carried with Dr. Vinson, Mr. Little, Dr. Douzart, Mrs. Smith-Iverson, and Mrs. Tharpe opposed. Ms. Green was not present at the vote.

 

ANNOUNCEMENTS. None

. 

ADJOURNMENT. Mr. Rachal moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to adjourn.  Vote on the motion carried, with Ms. Green not present at the vote, and the April 18, 2023, CPSB Meeting adjourned at approximately 10:30 p.m.
