9.04 Superintendent’s Report – Clarification of the City of Shreveport’s cameras located in the school zones Dr. Goree stated that Board Member Rachal requested clarification of the City of Shreveport’s cameras in Caddo school zones. He explained that in the 2022-2023 school year, the City of Shreveport entered into a contract with Blue Line Solutions giving Blue Line Solutions the authority to provide school zone speed enforcement.  He added that from the time that actually happened to now, the Caddo Parish School System has no connection with that contract.  Caddo Parish Schools do not receive any funds from that, nor do Caddo Parish Schools have any authority when it comes to any enforcement of speed zones dealing with the streets. Dr. Goree further stated that it is important to note and continue to reiterate that we are not involved in that. If there are any questions or concerns, they should be directed to the City of Shreveport Police Department.  The City has set up a Speed Zone Enforcement Division to address any questions or concerns. Dr. Goree closed with Caddo has no decision-making power in the manner this is governed by the Shreveport Police Department.  

 

9.05 Superintendent’s Report  - Possible 2023-2024 School Consolidations Dr. Goree stated that Board Member Rachal requested a discussion about school configurations as we go into next year.  He added that one of the most challenging conversations you can have is around school consolidations.  Dr. Goree acknowledged our friends from E. B. Williams Stoner Hill Laboratory School seated in the audience and stated he plans to visit with the staff.  He explained that there are concerns with E. B. Williams Stoner Hill Elementary School as you look at the percentage of students and the decline of students in the school. Dr. Goree agreed that the school does an exceptional job with special education students; however, the numbers have dropped to a historic low. He added that due to the low numbers, there are extreme issues with attracting certified teachers to the school, and currently, approximately 15% of the staff are fully certified teachers. As a Board, we value this as an opportunity for children. Dr. Goree said E.B. Williams Stoner Hill Elementary has only one teacher per grade level. Several of our Board Members have experience as educators and genuinely value the importance of a collaborative team when multiple people work within a grade level.  He added that as we go into next year, there are some absolute concerns with opportunities in that area and how we can best support children.  Dr. Goree explained that this conversation is not from a fiscal perspective, not even from an operations perspective though the numbers are low, but the opportunity to provide all of those students with highly qualified certified teachers. Dr. Goree closed and stated there would be conversations that staff would develop as we head into the spring budget cycle.
