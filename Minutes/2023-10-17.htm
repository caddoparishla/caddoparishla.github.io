October 17, 2023

CPSB Meeting

 

Members presentSteve Umling, Jasmine Green, Dr. Terence Vinson, Don Little,  Dr. Bonita Douzart, Mary Trammel, Darrin Dixon, Christine Tharpe, Barry Rachal, Katie McLain, Jessica Yeates, and Dottie Bell. Also present were Dr. T. Lamar Goree, Secretary, and Mr. Reggie Abrams, Board Counsel.

 

President Dr. Terence Vinson called the October 17, 2023, meeting to order, and Board Member Katie McLain gave the Invocation and led the Pledge of Allegiance.

 

CONSIDERATION OF THE MINUTES OF THE SEPTEMBER 5, 2023, and SEPTEMBER 19, 2023, CPSB MEETINGS Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mrs. McLain, to approve the minutes of the September 5, 2023, and September 19, 2023, CPSB meetings as submitted in the electronic mailout.  Vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

PRESENTATIONS AND RECOGNITIONS Ms. Marquel Sennet, Director of Communications, introduced Natalie Barker, a junior at Caddo Parish Magnet High School.  Natalie is a member of the Talented Arts Program and has the honor of being the only Louisiana Music Education Association All-State Jazz Ensemble Vocalist.  She performed two songs, “Once in a while” and “Charade”.

 

Ms. Sennet announced Caddo Parish Middle Magnet was recognized on September 19th by the U.S. Department of Education as a 2023 National Blue Ribbon School.  This recognition is based on a school’s overall academic performance or progress in closing achievement gaps among student groups on assessments. She added this announcement marks the third time CMM has received the nation’s highest honor and the 17th award for Caddo Schools, solidifying Caddo’s status as one of the leading districts in Louisiana for National Blue Ribbon Schools. 

 

Next, Ms. Sennet introduced 15 Caddo students that earned the distinctive honor as 2023-2024 National Merit Semifinalists – Vasu Arora, Robert Auchard, Sophie Chen, Hudson Kate Roberts, Hailey Huguet, Ruby Knoebel, Reynard Landreneau, Logan O’Callaghan, Marshall O’Callaghan, Jack Randall, Mason Sater, Logan Tais, Anjali Veerareddy, Phoebe Voumard, and Guanjun Wang.  These students are among a nationwide pool of semifinalists representing less than one percent of U. S. High School Seniors.

 

To conclude our presentations and recognitions for today, Ms. Sennet introduced Mrs. Pateece Davis-Hardy, CTA Program Administrator and Human Resource Specialist. Mrs. Davis-Hardy explained that the Caddo Teaching Academy is an innovative certification program within Caddo Public Schools, and the following educators are Caddo Teaching Academy’s graduates: Emily Brown, Booke Collier, Michkayla Coleman, Deana Elliott, Eumarcus Hicks, Cassidy Mainiero, Patrick McDade, Jeremy Newton, and Carla White. Congratulations to all of the graduates.

 

VISITORS The following visitors addressed the Board on matters of interest/concern:  Cynthia Green, Patsy Solomon, Martha Glover, Karen White, Jon Glover, and Jackie Lansdale.

 

ESTABLISH THE AGENDA AND PROPOSED CONSENT AGENDA Superintendent Goree highlighted items for the Board’s consideration.  President Vinson announced the following items are on consent: 6.02-6.03, 7.01-7.02, 8.01-8.02, 8.04, and 13.03.

 

Ms Trammel moved, seconded by Mrs. Yeates, to establish the agenda and consent agenda for the October 17, 2023, CPSB meeting as presented. Vote on the motion carried unanimously.

 

CONFIRM THE CONSENT AGENDA Mr. Dixon moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to confirm the consent agenda for the October 17, 2023, CPSB Meeting.  The vote on the motion carried,  with Ms. Green not present at vote. The following summarizes the Board’s action on the consent agenda items.

 

6.02 Personnel Leaves  The Board approved the Personnel Leaves as recommended by the Superintendent and submitted in the electronic mailout

 

6.03 Personnel Resignations The Board approved the Personnel Resignations as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

7.01 Purchasing Bid The Board approved the following bid as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout on the scoring tabulation sheet: RFP 01-24 – Property and Casualty Insurance Broker awarded to Alliant Insurance Services, Inc.

 

7.02 Capital Projects BidThe Board approved the bid as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout on the bid tabulation sheets: "Captain Shreve HVAC, Phase 2", Project 2025-751, be awarded to Hamm Mechanical (Not OCC*), Shreveport, LA with a Base Bid, Alternate 1, Alternate 2, and Alternate 3 for the sum total of $1,978,000.00. The budget amount is $2,000,000.00. Charge to Bond Funds.

 

8.01 Property/Bus Rental Requests The Board approved property/bus rental request as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.02 Capital Projects & Professional Designers for 2025-25 SY The Board Capital Projects & Professional Designers for 2024-2025 SY as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.04 Approval of the 2023-2024 Caddo Parish Pupil Progression Plan The Board approved the 2023-2024 Caddo Parish Pupil Progression Plan as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

13.03 Request for Settlement Authority Claimant: Mikayla Coleman, Date of Loss: 01/31/2018, Claim #6634395, Suit Number 613,906-B  The Board approved Request for Settlement Authority Claimant: Mikayla Coleman, Date of Loss: 01/31/2018, Claim #6634395, Suit Number 613,906-B, as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

 

8.03 Approval of Resolution Authorizing the Retention of Special Counsel for representation in the Social Media Litigation and authorizing the Execution of Attorney-Client Fee Contract with Ron Austin of Ron Austin Law  Mrs. Bell moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal to approve the Resolution authorizing the retention of Special Counsel for representation in the Social Media Litigation and authorizing the Execution of Attorney-Client Fee Contract with Ron Austin of Ron Austin Law as submitted in the electronic mailout. After discussion, Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Mrs. Bell, to call for the question to end the debate.  The vote on the motion failed, with Ms. Green, Dr. Vinson, Dr. Douzart, Mrs. Tharpe, Mrs. McLain, and Mrs. Yeates opposed.  The discussion continued.  Mrs. Yeates moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel, to end the debate.  The vote on the motion carried with Dr. Vinson and Katie McLain opposed, and the debate ended.  The vote on the original motion carried, with Mr. Umling, Dr. Vinson, Mr. Little, and Mrs. Tharpe opposed.  

 

13.01 Student Readmission Appeal Hearings Mr. Umling moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to approve staff recommendations for students HL, TF, and MM as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout – parents/guardians are in agreement.  The vote on the motion carried, with Mr. Dixon and Mrs. Bell not present at vote.

 

13.02 Litigation Update: Shannon Cox vs Caddo Parish School Board, Case No. 20-cv-1442, U. S. District Court for the Western District of Louisiana Shreveport Division After discussion, Mr. Umling moved, seconded by Mr. Rachal, to authorize resolution of the matter in accordance with the recommendations of staff and Board Counsel. The vote on the motion carried, with Mr. Dixon and Mrs. Bell not present at vote.

 

ANNOUNCEMENTS  Mrs. McLain shared that during her campaign last year, her son was diagnosed with epilepsy.  She announced that today marks the one-year anniversary of him being seizure-free.

 

ADJOURNMENT Ms. Trammel moved, seconded by Ms.Green, to adjourn.  The vote on the motion carried, with Mr. Dixon not present at vote. The October 17, 2023, CPSB Meeting adjourned at approximately 6:05 p.m.
