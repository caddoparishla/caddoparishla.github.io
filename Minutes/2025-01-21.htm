CPSB Meeting

January 21, 2025

 

The Caddo Parish School Board met on January 21, 2025, at 4:30 p.m. in the board room at 1961 Midway, Shreveport, LA. Board members present were Steve Umling, Jasmine Green, Dr. Terence Vinson, Don Little, Dr. Bonita Douzart,

Mary Trammel, Christine Tharpe, Jerry Bowman Jr, Barry Rachal, Katie McLain, Jessica Yeates, and Dottie Bell. Superintendent Keith Burton and Legal Counsel Reginald Abrams were also present.

 

The meeting was called to order at 4:30 p.m.

12.01 Board Member Announcements and Requests

Dr. Douzart stated we are celebrating Martin Luther King, Jr. with an absentee and culminate by saying, "No lie can live forever; truth crushed to earth will rise again; the arc of the moral universe is long, but it bends toward justice" and "our lives begin to end the day we become silent about things that matter" – all of these quoted by Martin Luther King, Jr. Mr. Little said the Bible says those that endure to the end will be saved, and we made it. He thanked Superintendent Burton for his words and for listening with open ears, which showed great character and humility. Mr. Little asked that the presentation on the new system being implemented be posted on the CPSB website for those who could not attend. Ms. Trammel reminded the board about the City Wide Day of Prayer at Peaceful Rest Missionary Baptist Church on January 27, 2025, at noon. Mr. Umling said the town hall meetings that Mr. Burton hosted were great. He said we must fix the loophole problem of putting items on the agenda and establish a policy to address this issue. Mrs. Bell suggested that the board's conversation during this meeting should have been discussed during the policy review committee held earlier today. Ms. Green asked the board to pray for family of Mark Milam, former School Board Representative, who recently passed away. Ms. Green announced she attended the mid-year graduation at North Caddo High School. She thanked Mr. Gibbs and Mr. Mainiero for coming to help her when she ran out of gas. She added congratulations to the graduates at North Caddo High School. Ms. Green pointed out that she abstained on the vote for the preK-8 academies because she had more questions. But, for the record she stated she is in full support of the K-8 Academies.
