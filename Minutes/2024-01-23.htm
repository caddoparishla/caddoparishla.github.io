8.06 Consideration to approve the recommended changes to the Caddo Parish School Board Health, Dental, Life, and Disability Plans. Members of the audience voiced their concerns and opinions regarding this agenda item: Jon Glover - opposed to the insurance premium increase; Jordan Thomas (RRU)- supports the proposal for 7.5% premium increase, no increase in deductibles, $7M from the General Fund; Annalee Yard-concerned with insurance changes for a retiree on a fixed income; Marsha Whitford - opposed to insurance premium increase; Mary Beth James, retired bus driver, opposed to insurance.

President Yeates explained the procedure that would be used to vote for the insurance options.    Five options for the 2024-2025 plan year are listed as possible insurance options. We will vote on each option, beginning with Option 1.  All Board Members can vote on each option.  The first option that receives a majority vote of the Board Members present and voting will approve the option.  If no option gets a majority vote, the board will consider the next options, receiving the most votes in their favor.  The process will continue until an option gets a majority vote.

After a lengthy discussion, Dr. Vinson moved, seconded by Ms. Green, to overrule the President’s procedure to vote for the insurance options. The vote on the motion failed, with Mr. Umling, Mr. Little, Ms. Trammel, Mr. Dixon, Mrs. Tharpe, Mrs. McLain, Mrs. Yeates, and Mrs. Bell opposed.  The discussion continued.

Mr. Little moved, seconded by Mrs. Bell, to call for the question to end the discussion.  The vote on the motion carried with Ms. Green and Mrs. McClain opposed.

President Yeates called for a 10-minute recess at 6:10 p.m.  President Yeates called the meeting to order at 6:20 p.m.

Option 1:  Mr. Little moved, seconded by Mrs. Tharpe for Option 1 (Increase deductibles and max out-of-pocket costs; Increase premiums by 15%).  The vote was as follows: Yes (2) - Mr. Little and Mrs. Tharpe. No (9) - Mr. Umling, Ms. Green, Dr. Vinson, Dr. Douzart, Ms. Trammel, Mr. Dixon, Mrs. McLain, Mrs. Yeates, and Mrs. Bell.  Option 1 failed with no majority vote.

Option 2:  Mrs. McLain moved, seconded by Dr. Douzart for Option 2 (Increase premiums 15%; $3 million transfer from General Fund). The vote was as follows: Yes (4) – Mr. Little, Mrs. Tharpe, Mrs. McLain, and Mrs. Yeates. No (7) – Mr. Umling, Ms. Green, Dr. Vinson, Dr. Douzart, Ms. Trammel, Mr. Dixon, and Mrs. Bell. Option 2 failed with no majority vote.

Option 3:  Mr. Dixon moved, seconded by Ms. Trammel for Option 3 (Increase deductibles, max out-of-pocket costs; Increase premiums 7.5%; $7M transfer from General Fund).  The vote was as follows: Yes (3) – Mr. Little, Ms. Trammel, Mr. Dixon. No (8) – Mr. Umling, Ms. Green, Dr. Vinson, Dr. Douzart, Mrs. Tharpe, Mrs. McLain, Mrs. Yeates, and Mrs. Bell. Option 3 failed with no majority vote.

Option 4: Increase deductibles and Max out of Pocket Costs by 50% of recommended increase; increase premiums by 7.5%; $8.5M transfer from General Fund—no motion submitted from the Board. No vote.

Option 5:  Dr. Vinson moved, seconded by Mrs. Bell for Option 5 (Increase premiums by 7.5%; $11.5M transfer from General Fund). The vote was as follows: Yes (7) – Mr. Umling, Ms. Green, Dr. Vinson, Dr. Douzart, Ms. Trammel, Mr. Dixon, and Mrs. Bell. No (4) Mr. Little, Mrs. Tharpe, Mrs. McLain, Mrs. Yeates.  Option 5 carried with a majority vote.

 

8.07 Charter School Application for Northwest Louisiana Classical Academy (Charter Schools USA) Jon Glover spoke in opposition to the approval of the charter school application.
