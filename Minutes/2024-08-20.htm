August 20, 2024

CPSB Meeting

 

 

The Caddo Parish School Board (CPSB) met on August 20, 2024, at 4:30 p.m. in the board room at 1961 Midway, Shreveport, Louisiana.  Board members present were Steve Umling, Jasmine Green, Don Little, Dr. Bonita Douzart, Mary Trammel, Darrin Dixon,

Christine Tharpe, Barry Rachal, Katie McLain, and Dottie Bell.  Interim Superintendent Leisa Woolfolk and Legal Counsel Reginald Abrams were also present.

1.01 Invocation (Christine Tharpe, District 8)

1.02 Pledge of Allegiance (A. C. Steere students)

 

1.03 National Anthem (Mr. Charles LaFollette, Assistant Principal - Green Oaks High School)

 

1.04 Roll Call Dottie Bell, 1st Vice President, called the meeting to order at 4:30 p.m.

 

2.01 Consideration of the Minutes of the July 16, 2024 CPSB Meeting Move to approve the minutes of the July 16, 2024 CPSB Meeting as recommended by staff and submitted in the electronic mailout.

Motion by Darrin Dixon, second by Don Little

Final Resolution: Motion Carries

Yes: Steve Umling, Jasmine Green, Don Little, Dr. Bonita Douzart, Mary Trammel, Darrin Dixon, Christine Tharpe, Barry Rachal, Katie McLain, Dottie Bell

 

3.01 Presentations and Recognitions 1) Musical Presentation 2) Medal of Heroism 3) 2024-2025 New Administrative Appointments

 

3.02 Visitors Marvin Earl Fletcher explained that there was an organization for young men to become leaders in the community. He passed out informational flyers. Jon Glover stated she was against the new CTE Center Resolution that the board approved. Allen

Arkansas, a Walnut Hill parent, stated his son was retaliated against when the dad reported an incident at school.  Jason Frazier informed the board that his wife is a substitute teacher who worked all last year and should get the bonus.  Marsha Winfred, a

retired teacher, said retirees have a vested interest in the new Superintendent.  Stephanie Sicard, a teacher for 30 years in Caddo Parish, is a member of LRTA and CRTA and wants a local candidate chosen for Superintendent because she feels we need

someone who has instructional knowledge and flexibility.  Sally Cox stated the new Superintendent needs to develop a plan to stop students from going to other parishes because she feels we are losing middle-class students to other parishes.  Jackie Lansdale

voiced her concern regarding security coordinators having to wait too long for their first check.
